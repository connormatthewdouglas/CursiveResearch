## Key Additions Imported into Chapter 14

- Stronger distinction between demonstrated localized outer-loop self-optimization and speculative unbounded recursive self-improvement.
- Expanded taxonomy: parameter fine-tuning loops, discrete scaffolding optimization, programmatic skill acquisition, runtime policy modification, algorithmic superoptimization, and parameter/model merging.
- Additional systems: Programmatic Skill Networks, Gödel Agent, Polaris, SICA, Process-Based Self-Rewarding, Darwin Gödel Machine, CodeEvolve, Noise-to-Meaning RSI, TerraLingua, and open-ended AI safety literature.
- Stronger emphasis that the verifier/fitness function is the controlling variable in any self-improving organism.
- Stronger failure-mode coverage: evaluator drift, Goodharting, hidden regressions, fake progress, sandbox escape, unauthorized resource escalation, and over-complex multi-agent scaffolds.
- Research conclusion that software-organism improvement should begin with bounded, externally verified, execution-grounded loops rather than unconstrained self-modification.
